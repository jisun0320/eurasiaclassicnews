Why Did the Stuttgart Philharmonic Choose Edwin E.S. Kim for Its Korean Debut?

For the Stuttgart Philharmonic Orchestra's first-ever tour of Korea, internationally acclaimed violinist Edwin E.S. Kim took center stage in Beethoven's Violin Concerto in D Major, Op. 61.

Known for his collaborations with major orchestras across Europe and Asia, Kim delivered a performance marked by exceptional artistry, depth, and musical insight. His refined interpretation and seamless collaboration with the Stuttgart Philharmonic earned enthusiastic applause and became one of the highlights of the orchestra's historic Korean debut.

"Edwin E.S. Kim is a musician who truly understands the essence of music. He brought remarkable depth, maturity, and authenticity to Beethoven's concerto."

Adrian Prabava, conductor
By Ji Sun Lee, Editor  ·  Daejeon  ·  28 May
